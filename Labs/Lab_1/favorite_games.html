Easily the game I've invested the most time in across all the various platforms it's been on in past decade. From pocket edition to Xbox 360 edition to PC to Xbox One to PC again, it never gets old. Despite everything that's been added over the years, the basic formula of it hasn't changed all that much, and I think that's part of its charm. It can be a simple survival game, it can be one of the most complex engineering games if that's your cup of tea, or it can be a sandbox for creatives to flourish. And that's not to mention its thriving modding community that adds even more variety in the various versions that exist.

Omori is definitely one of the more unique RPGs I've played ever. I was initially drawn to it by its sketch art style, creator's collaboration with a V-Tuber group I'm a fan of, and through a close friend's recommendation. What I got when I played was, yes, great visuals, but also a deep, emotional story that kept me invested throughout my journey, a fantastic soundtrack that I still listen to occassionally outside of the game, and a combat system that utilzies a unique mechanic in the form of emotions that I've seen few, if any, other games use in the way Omori does.

The internet has sung its praise for this game enough, especially with the way its sequel maintained a cult-like following up until its release last year. Can't really blame them either, as the original is a fantastic Metroidvania, probably the best I've played to-date. Its setting really sets the tone of desolateness and mystery as you maneuver through Hallownest; its soundtrack supporting that tone while journeying, but drastically ramping up in intensity when boss fights occur. Customization with charms allow for lots of different playstyles, which I'm fond of.
